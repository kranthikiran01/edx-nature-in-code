What is evolution?

Evolution is the change in allele frequencies of a population over time.

The Hardy–Weinberg principle, also known as the Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium, model, theorem, or law, states that allele and genotype frequencies in a population will remain constant from generation to generation in the absence of other evolutionary influences. The evolutionary influences being :
  • Genetic drift
  • Mutation
  • Migration and
  • Natural selection
